Primary Source

Definition: "Primary sources are materials produced by people or groups directly involved in the event or topic under consideration, either as participants or witnesses... Some primary wources are written documents, such as letters; diaries; newspaper and magazine articles; speeches; autobiographies; treatises; census data; and marriage, birth, and death registers."

Letter Speech Book Telegram
Court Document Chart Newspaper Advertisement
Press Release Memorandum Report Painting
Photo Film Map Other

Basic questions to answer from Primary Sources:

  1. Who created the source?
  2. Who was the intended audience?
  3. When is it from?
  4. Where is it from?
  5. What is it talking about
  6. What was happening at the time in history this source was created?
  7. What does this tell you about the person(s) who produced it?

Secondary Sources

Definition: “…secondary sources are texts – such as books, articles, [and academic websites]…that are written or created by people who were not eyewitnesses to the events or period in question; instead, the authors of secondary sources synthesize, analyze, and interpret primary sources.”
